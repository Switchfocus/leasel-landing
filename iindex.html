§ 01 · The situation Memo
FromPierre Anisimov
To_______________________
DateApril 2026
ReLeasel — why now, why this

Luxembourg has the tightest rental market in Western Europe. Vacancy under 2 percent. A well-priced apartment receives 15 to 20 applications inside 48 hours, and is gone inside a week. Most renters lose by being slower than the next applicant, not by being a worse fit.

The renters lining up are not local. 47 percent of the workforce crosses a border each morning. Another 47 percent of residents are foreign. Most arrive without a French CDI, without a Luxembourgish payslip history, without a network. They re-compile the same dossier — five PDFs, three languages, one cover letter — for every single viewing.

No tenant-owned, portable, verified rental dossier exists in Luxembourg, in Belgium, or in Germany. France has DossierFacile (state-run, 250,000 accounts). The UK has Canopy. Continental Europe — nothing.

That is the gap. This document is what I plan to do about it.

Pierre — Cessange

~280,000renters · LU

Approximate Luxembourg rental population — residents in non-owner-occupied housing plus the rolling pool of cross-border workers actively considering relocation. Homeownership has fallen from 72.4% in 2022 to 63.5% in 2024. The renter base is structurally growing, every year, by policy-resistant immigration. A small absolute market. A near-perfect lab.

§ 04 · The product Three verbs

Leasel — a verified rental dossier you actually own.

01Build
A guided form. Identity, income, employment, references. What the law allows. Nothing more.
02Verify
Documents checked through certified KYC partners and, optionally, LuxTrust. The dossier is stamped, not just stored.
03Share
A single revocable link per application. Time-limited, audit-logged, shown to the agency, owned by the renter.

§ 07 · Business model Three streams

Two-sided marketplace. B2B-led.

  1. Agency SaaS
    €200–500/month per agency seat. Pre-verified dossiers flow into the agency's CRM. Hours saved per applicant, quantifiable from week one.
    Phase 10–9 months · primary revenue
  2. Per-verified-dossier fee
    €3–5 per dossier delivered into an agency's pipeline. Scales linearly with agency adoption; minimal marginal cost.
    Phase 29–18 months · margin expansion
  3. Tenant premium
    €5–10/month for advanced sharing analytics, group dossiers, deposit insurance bundling. Free tier remains free, forever.
    Phase 318 months+ · flywheel revenue
/* Agency SaaS is the fastest path to revenue. Tenant premium follows traction. Anyone selling this consumer-first is solving the cold-start the wrong way around. */
